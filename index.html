Quick Reference — How This Works
1
Paste the patient message
Drop the patient's current message into the left box. If it's a reply to something you already sent, click Add Prior Context and paste the earlier thread there first.
2
Click Run Triage
The AI reads the message against your Knowledge Base and returns a classification, routing decision, and a draft response. Usually takes 5–10 seconds.
3
Check the results top to bottom
Classification card — urgency and category. Routing card — only appears when something needs to go to another team; copy the internal note into Bask. Generated Response — copy and send to patient.
4
Teach the AI if you edited
If you changed the draft before sending, paste what you actually sent into What was sent to the patient and click Submit & Learn. Only do this when you made changes.
Reading the Results
The top card shows what the AI decided: urgency (Routine / Same Day / URGENT), task type, clinical category, and severity badge if a side effect was detected. Check this first. If anything looks wrong, correct the category using the pills and save.
Side Effect: Severe means escalate to a provider immediately. Side Effect: Moderate means gather more information and reassess. Side Effect: Mild means an education response is appropriate.
Appears only when non-clinical items are detected. Shows what needs to go to the support team and contains an Internal Note for Staff — paste this into the Bask chat thread when routing.
Routine means respond in normal queue order. Same Day means the patient needs a reply today. URGENT means escalate now. If wrong, adjust the dropdown and click Save.
Using Prior Context
Any time the patient message is a reply to something you already sent. Without the earlier thread, the AI is triaging a fragment. Click Add Prior Context, paste everything that came before, then paste only their latest reply in the main message box.
No. For a patient's first message — one that isn't a reply to anything — leave Prior Context empty.
Teaching the AI
After sending your response, if you changed the draft, paste what you actually sent and click Submit & Learn. The AI compares the two versions and saves a learning note. If you sent the draft verbatim, skip this entirely.
Click the correct category pill in the Classification card (Clinical or Non-Clinical), then click Save. Each correction is stored and builds a dataset that improves the AI's classification accuracy over time.
Go to Clinical Knowledge Base → Corrections tab and click Load Response Corrections. Find the incorrect entry and click Delete.
Knowledge Base
Everything the AI reads before responding to a patient message — protocols, side effect guidance, routing rules, urgency thresholds, and response templates. Any change takes effect immediately on the next triage.
Side Effects — education and management guidance.
Templates — response frameworks for common messages.
Protocols — if/then decision logic.
Rules & Notes — urgency thresholds, severity rules. Edit carefully.
Routing — when and how to escalate or route.
URLs — links you reference in patient responses.
Common Mistakes
Triaging a reply without Prior Context — the AI gets a fragment and produces a generic response.

Ignoring the red severity badge — Severe means escalate to a provider now.

Ignoring the routing card — if a non-clinical item is flagged, the internal note needs to go into Bask.

Entering the wrong thing in "What was sent to the patient" — this teaches the AI incorrect behavior. Delete it in the Corrections tab immediately.
